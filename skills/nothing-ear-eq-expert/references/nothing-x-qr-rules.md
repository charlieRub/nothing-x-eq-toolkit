# Nothing X QR Rules

## Compatibility guardrails

Nothing X may reject a QR even when a generic QR decoder can read it. The critical rule found in this project: each of the 8 EQ bands must stay inside its original frequency range.

| Band | Valid range | Safe working zone |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 20-100 Hz | 45-70 Hz |
| 2 | 100-200 Hz | 115-160 Hz |
| 3 | 200-400 Hz | 260-330 Hz |
| 4 | 400-1000 Hz | 600-800 Hz |
| 5 | 1000-3000 Hz | 1800-2600 Hz |
| 6 | 3000-6000 Hz | 4000-5200 Hz |
| 7 | 6000-12000 Hz | 8000-10000 Hz |
| 8 | 12000-20000 Hz | 14500-16000 Hz |

Do not place vocal clarity at 2800 Hz in band 6 or air at 8500 Hz in band 8. Move the idea to the nearest valid band instead.

## Safe numeric ranges

- Gain: stay mostly between -3.0 dB and +4.5 dB. Use +5 dB or +6 dB only for intentionally heavy bass profiles.
- Q: prefer 0.8-2.2. Avoid extreme Q unless solving a precise harshness or mud issue.
- Names: keep short and simple. Prefer ASCII or plain UTF-8 text without emojis, slashes, or symbols.

## Payload format

The importable QR payload is Base64 text. It is generated by gzip-compressing this binary structure:

```text
00 60
8 bands:
  gain float32 little-endian
  frequency float32 little-endian
  Q float32 little-endian
01
name_length_bytes
name_utf8
```

The QR encodes only the Base64 string. Bass Enhance, ANC, Personal Sound, volume, and codec settings are separate app settings and must be recommended in text.

## QR rendering

- Use black modules on a white background.
- Use a large quiet zone: margin 4 minimum, margin 8 preferred.
- Use clean PNG for import. Decorative posters are secondary.
- Use error correction `M` or `H`; if the QR is dense, prefer a shorter name and margin 8.
- Verify with a QR decoder before delivery, then test in Nothing X when possible.

## Failure diagnosis

If Nothing X says `Codigo QR valido no encontrado`:

1. Check every frequency against the band table.
2. Shorten the name.
3. Regenerate with margin 8, scale 18, black-on-white.
4. Avoid logos or overlays.
5. Use the clean PNG rather than a screenshot, poster, or chat-compressed crop.
